Model Formulation
The offline RL agent is trained using Conservative Q-Learning with a Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR) heuristic penalty to strictly minimize downside volatility.
Reward Shaping (VaR Penalty):
$$ R_t = r_t - \lambda \cdot \max(0, \text{VaR}_{95}(R_{trail}) - r_{trail}) $$
Hierarchical LLM Constraint:
$$ \pi(a|s) \text{ s.t. } \sum_{i} w_i \le \text{Cap}_{LLM} $$

Performance Analysis & Insights

Hierarchical Advantage: The Hierarchical LLM+RL models (LLM+CQL and LLM+TD3BC) demonstrate superior downside risk mitigation compared to their vanilla counterpart models. By utilizing the LLM Strategist's macro regime classification to apply dynamic sector exposure caps and cash safe-haven rules during high-risk regimes (like COVID Crash 2020 and Rate Hike Choppy 2022), they significantly reduce the Maximum Drawdown (Max DD).

CQL vs TD3+BC: Conservative Q-Learning (CQL) maintains a more defensive, conservative risk profile by incorporating a conditional value-at-risk (CVaR) reward penalty, while TD3+BC achieves slightly higher raw returns in steady trending environments (like Quiet Bull 2017) at the cost of higher drawdowns and volatility.

Key Takeaways & Improvement Areas: Adding structural risk-limiting rules successfully guards the RL agent's weight allocations against black-swan macro environments. Future model iterations could incorporate real-time spread commissions and attention-based shared encoder modules to capture inter-asset correlations more effectively.
